# ADR-002: Self-Service Business Registration with Admin Payment Gate

## Status

Accepted

## Date

2026-06-10

## Context

The original archived PRD assumed admin-operated business profiles — the admin team would create each business, set their discount terms, and manage all content. User research (conversations with business owners) indicated that businesses want control over their own profiles. They need to update photos, descriptions, social media links, opening hours, and coupon terms on their own schedule without involving an admin. At the same time, since subscription billing is managed externally (no payment gateway in V1), admin needs a simple on/off switch to disable businesses that stop paying.

## Decision

Business owners register themselves via a self-service flow. Once approved, they manage their own profile (logo, description, social media, opening hours, online menu link) and create/manage their own coupons and discounts. Admin retains the ability to enable or disable any business, serving as the payment gate (active = paid, inactive = unpaid).

## Alternatives Considered

### Alternative 1: Admin-Operated Business Profiles

- **Description**: Admin team creates all business profiles and manages content. Businesses receive login credentials and access only the validation dashboard.
- **Pros**: Simpler V1 build (no self-service registration or profile management UI needed), consistent content quality, admin controls all discount terms.
- **Cons**: Admin bottleneck for every content update, slower iteration for businesses, does not scale to multi-neighborhood without dedicated ops staff.
- **Why rejected**: Creates an operational bottleneck that contradicts the goal of building a self-sustaining neighborhood network. Businesses reported wanting control.

### Alternative 2: Full Self-Service with Tiered Subscription and In-App Payment

- **Description**: Self-service registration, profile management, coupon creation, plus tiered subscription plans (free basic vs paid premium) with in-app payment.
- **Pros**: Most complete experience, monetization built into the product flow, no external billing dependency.
- **Cons**: Requires payment gateway integration (PCI scope, webhook handling, refund logic) — adds weeks to V1 timeline. Businesses reported disliking additional transaction fees.
- **Why rejected**: Payment gateway is unnecessary complexity for V1. Flat subscription billed externally is simpler and validated by user research.

## Consequences

### Positive

- Businesses can update their own content on their schedule
- Admin workload reduced to enabling/disabling based on payment
- Self-service scales to multi-neighborhood without proportional ops growth
- Businesses can experiment with coupon terms independently

### Negative

- Higher V1 build complexity: business registration flow, profile management UI, coupon management UI
- Risk of inconsistent or low-quality business profiles (poor photos, incomplete descriptions)
- Businesses may misunderstand the admin enable/disable flow if they stop paying

### Risks

- **Profile quality**: Some businesses may not fill out their profiles well. Mitigation: require minimum fields (logo, description, category) for profile to be visible; show templates/examples during onboarding.
- **Payment confusion**: If a business's access is disabled for non-payment, they may not understand why. Mitigation: send email notification with clear reason and reactivation instructions before disabling.

## Implementation Notes

- Business registration flow must collect: name, CNPJ, category, email, password, logo upload. Admin reviews and enables the business after confirming payment (external).
- Post-activation, business dashboard includes: edit profile (logo, description, social media, opening hours, menu link), manage coupons (create/edit/deactivate), view validation history.
- Admin panel: list all businesses with active/inactive toggle. When toggled inactive, business dashboard and coupons become inaccessible; catalog hides the business.
