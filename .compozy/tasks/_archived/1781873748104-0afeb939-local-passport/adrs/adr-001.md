# ADR-001: Single-Neighborhood Pilot Before Multi-Neighborhood Expansion

## Status

Accepted

## Date

2026-06-05

## Context

The Passaporte Local idea originally targeted a single neighborhood (Jurerê, Florianópolis). The user expressed interest in launching across multiple neighborhoods simultaneously to maximize learning surface area and spread brand awareness faster. The council debate surfaced counterarguments from The Product Mind and The Devil's Advocate: multi-neighborhood launch confounds retention metrics, multiplies the manual verification bottleneck, and makes it harder to isolate the core retention loop before investing in scale. The Pragmatic Engineer confirmed that the current stack (Deno KV, single admin) is adequate for single-neighborhood density but would require operational scaling (automated verification, multi-neighborhood admin tooling) before expanding horizontally.

## Decision

Launch V1 in a single dense neighborhood (starting with Jurerê). Achieve validated retention metrics before expanding to additional neighborhoods. Keep manual document verification for V1 but instrument approval time as a core KPI and plan to add automated verification before multi-neighborhood expansion.

## Alternatives Considered

### Alternative 1: Multi-Neighborhood Launch From Day One

- **Description**: Launch simultaneously across 3-5 neighborhoods in Florianópolis.
- **Pros**: Faster brand awareness, larger total user base, more data variety
- **Cons**: Confounded retention metrics, manual verification bottleneck × number of neighborhoods, higher customer acquisition cost spread thin, harder to isolate which variables drive success
- **Why rejected**: The retention loop (discovery → credentialing → redemption → repeat) must be proven before scaling. Multi-neighborhood data is confounded and does not validate unit economics.

### Alternative 2: Fully Automated Verification Before Launch

- **Description**: Build automated document verification (OCR + fraud detection SDKs) before any user sign-up.
- **Pros**: Scalable from day one, no manual bottleneck
- **Cons**: 2-4 weeks of engineering + API costs before validating demand; may solve a scaling problem that doesn't exist yet
- **Why rejected**: Deferred to Phase 2. Manual verification at single-neighborhood scale is manageable; the bottleneck only compounds with expansion.

## Consequences

### Positive

- Clear retention loop can be validated in isolation
- Lower operational complexity during critical learning phase
- Faster iteration per dollar of spend
- Team can build deep local relationships with pilot businesses

### Negative

- Delays brand awareness in other neighborhoods
- Risk of overfitting to one neighborhood's specific dynamics (income profile, foot traffic, merchant sophistication)
- Competitors may launch in other neighborhoods while we focus on one

### Risks

- **Overfitting to Jurerê**: Jurerê is a wealthy beach enclave; its dynamics may not generalize. Mitigation: document assumptions explicitly (income range, business density, foot traffic patterns) and track which break during expansion.
- **Competitor preemption**: A WhatsApp-based competitor could launch a simpler model elsewhere. Mitigation: speed is still critical — single-neighborhood pilot should aim for < 8 weeks to validated retention metrics.

## Implementation Notes

- Instrument approval time, resident activation rate (> 30%), and monthly validation count (target 200+) as core KPI dashboard
- Design data models and catalog to support multi-neighborhood from day one even though only one neighborhood is active
- Plan automated document verification implementation for Phase 2 immediately upon hitting retention targets
