# ADR-001: Scope and UX Decisions for V1 Resident Frontend

## Status

Accepted

## Date

2026-06-26

## Context

We are establishing the scope and key UX patterns for the Passaporte Local Resident Frontend (V1). The user experience must balance visual delight (evoking a Brazilian passport feel) with execution constraints, performance at physical points of sale (POS), accessibility (WCAG 2.2), and the operational capacity of local merchants.

We need to make specific decisions on:
1. The animation model for opening the digital passport.
2. The model for the Home Feed and social posts from merchants.
3. Access security and checkout validation states.

## Decision

We have decided to implement the following scope for V1:
1. **Simplified Passport Transition**: We will render the passport cover as a flat, premium Bento-style card utilizing deep blue gradients. Tapping the cover will open it using a lightweight, hardware-accelerated 2D slide-and-fade transition to reveal the light yellow inner pages containing the QR code, status, and active codes. We reject the complex 3D CSS card flip animation to avoid performance lags and focus traps at POS.
2. **Hybrid Social & System-Generated Feed**: The Home Feed will intersperse automated database event updates (e.g., new coupon releases, community transaction saving updates) with social posts (text + image updates) authored and published directly by local merchants.
3. **Merchant Social Publishing Tool**: We will build a simple merchant publishing interface and API endpoints for verified business accounts to post promotions and text/image announcements directly to the feed.
4. **Synchronous Validation with Client-Side Retries**: The validation will remain online via `POST /api/transactions/validate`. If a user is pending, the passport card will render in a locked state, blocking the opening action and showing a clear verification message.

## Alternatives Considered

### Alternative 1: Full 3D CSS Card-Flip Animation

- **Description**: Implement a 3D perspective flip showing the card flipping 180 degrees on tap.
- **Pros**: Higher visual novelty, matches the original physical passport metaphor.
- **Cons**: Prone to mobile Safari rendering stutter, complicates keyboard/screen reader focus states, slows down entry at checkout.
- **Why rejected**: The performance risk at a busy cashier register outweighs the aesthetic value.

### Alternative 2: Purely System-Generated (No Merchant Posts)

- **Description**: Keep the feed restricted to automated database log triggers only.
- **Pros**: Zero effort for merchants, simpler database schema, no media upload pipeline.
- **Cons**: Lacks community feeling, doesn't capture merchant-specific storytelling (like the "Banana Festival" post in Figma).
- **Why rejected**: Merchants need a direct channel to speak to residents and promote specific events/deals, making social content a key driver of engagement.

## Consequences

### Positive

- **Higher Engagement**: Residents see rich, authentic content (e.g. bakery daily specials, events) interspersed with transaction saving logs.
- **Direct Merchant-Resident Relationship**: Empowers local business owners to market directly to the neighborhood community.
- **POS Performance**: 2D transitions are reliable across low-end mobile devices.

### Negative

- **Increased Technical Complexity**: Requires building a publishing pipeline, image hosting/compression utility, and database schema for posts in V1.
- **Content Moderation Risk**: Opens the possibility of low-quality or inappropriate merchant uploads.

### Risks

- **Stale Social Feed**: If merchants are too busy to post, the feed might feel empty.
- **Mitigation**: Mix system-generated event updates (new coupons, resident savings) automatically to keep the feed constantly fresh even with low merchant posting frequency.
